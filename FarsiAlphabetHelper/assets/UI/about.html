Farsi (Persian) alphabet has 32 letters. 28 letters are exactly as Arabic alphabet, however, in Farsi most of these letters pronounce differently. The extra four letters in Farsi are ,پ, چ, ژ, گ. For example in Arabic there is no sound such as "pe". In Arabic "pe" (پ) is normally replaced by "fe" or "be". It is interesting to know that language Farsi, was actually always pronounced Parsi.
The letters, ت , ط are pronounce the same in Farsi (te), while in Arabic it has a different pronunciation. The letters ,ز, ذ, ظ, ض are also pronounce the same (ze), unlike Arabic. So you might wonder why Farsi speakers need so much ze? Well, Normally in pure Farsi words, you do not see letters such as: ط,ظ, ض. So probably Farsi speakers need these letters because there are lots of Arabic words, that have squeezed in their language.
In both Arabic and Farsi, there are no letters consider as vowels. Instead, for beginners (only in children books) some sounds are placed on or under the letters to make them readable. So how can you tell if it is pronounced "ba", "be" or "bo"? Well practice, you should learn the words by heart.
